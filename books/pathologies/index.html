Volume V

Pathologies

Failure modes, and how economists know what they know.

Forthcoming

Pathologies asks whether the things that break in an economy are defects of the machine or the machine working as designed: the phone that dies the month the new model ships, the scam nobody goes to prison for, the recovery that skips a street, the crash, the currency that dies on the news, the famine that strikes while granaries are full, the regime that tips over, and, in the end, how the discipline that studies all this knows anything at all. Each chapter takes one question and follows the evidence to an answer.

The first four chapters are available below; the rest are in preparation and will be released as the volume is written. Every concept is treated here once, and the other volumes point back rather than restate.
